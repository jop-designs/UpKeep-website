About UpKeep

A cultural shift in how we value bikes

UpKeep bicycles is an open source community for people who want longer bike life. We focus on parts that are often ignored until the whole bike feels degraded. Instead of waiting for breakdown, we fix value early.

What UpKeep is

A shared library of practical bicycle part designs and workshop methods. Not step-by-step repair manuals, but adaptable files and local support to help people make meaningful upgrades.

What UpKeep is not

We are not trying to replace bike mechanics. We focus on approachable parts that influence comfort, aesthetics, and emotional connection to a bike.

Mission

Every year, around 350,000 bicycles are removed from the streets in the Netherlands, most of them still rideable.

Our mission is to cut that number in half within the next five years, by shifting how people relate to their bikes: from something purely functional, to something worth looking after.

Philosophy

We believe the real problem isn't bicycle repair. It's that we've lost our connection to the things we own. When a bike starts to look tired, it starts to feel disposable. By the time it breaks, most people have already given up on it.

That's why UpKeep doesn't wait for breakdown. We focus on the parts that make a bike feel personal. When you've made something yourself, you take care of it differently.

How the project started

UpKeep began as a master's thesis in Industrial Design Engineering at TU Delft, born from a personal conviction: that making things, with your hands, together with others, is one of the most powerful ways to change our relationship with the products we use.

I believe that making and community and vital in changing our unsustainable relationship with products and I see it as a personal challenge to create more awareness around this topic as we are becoming surrounded with more an more mass produced products.

"Let's take care of the things we own."

Part Focus

Why these seven parts

They break first

These are the parts most likely to wear out or show signs of age over time.

They change how your bike feels

Worn out versions of these parts make a perfectly functional bike seem neglected long before it actually fails.

Anyone can make them

You can still ride to work without a bell or a rack, so there is no urgency. UpKeep is designed to meet people before their bike becomes a problem, not after.

They are visible

A handmade fender or a custom chain guard is something people notice. Lasting change in how we treat our possessions happens when people see others doing it too.
